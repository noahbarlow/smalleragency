01the challenge

building a food category from the ground up.

When Michael Summerfield approached Smaller, BestDish wasn't a company. It was an idea. The challenge wasn't simply creating a brand — it was designing an entirely new consumer behaviour: convincing someone to buy restaurant-quality meals from a freezer inside their own condominium.

We needed a new brand, a new retail experience, a new customer journey, a new trust model, and a completely new go-to-market strategy.

✕

not a delivery app

The market understood those. BestDish fit the category anyway — expensive, indifferent, margin-extracting.

✕

not frozen grocery

Mass-produced, no chef, no story. Everything the packaging had to argue against — from inside a freezer.

✕

not a vending machine

Snacks-only convenience without quality. The nearest mental model, and the most dangerous one.

every decision had to answer one question: how do you make something unfamiliar feel immediately obvious?

02our role

strategic brand partner, end to end.

Smaller shaped virtually every customer-facing aspect of the business — Brand → Package → Demand, run on a category that didn't exist yet.

brand strategy

Positioning BestDish around iconic restaurant meals rather than frozen food. Core narrative, personality, messaging, tone of voice.

brand identity

Logo, typography, colour palette, photography direction, illustration system, motion principles, brand guidelines.

packaging system

Packaging that communicates restaurant quality from inside a freezer — premium on the shelf, functional inside a complex food manufacturing process.

digital experience

Website strategy, UX and design. Information architecture, storytelling, open-pricing visualizations, restaurant pages, producer pages, chef profiles.

launch strategy

Not advertising — behaviour design. First-meal-free strategy, resident onboarding, launch events, sampling, building signage, welcome materials, referral concepts, loyalty thinking, the conversion journey.

social & content

Visual direction, launch photography, restaurant and producer storytelling, video concepts, resident education, brand voice, social strategy.

✳the identity system
03the packaging system

restaurant quality, argued from a freezer.

Each package had to look premium while functioning inside a complex food manufacturing process — the pack as the brand's most-touched media buy, working the three-second test through frosted glass.

  • meal sleeves
  • labels & nutritional layouts
  • information hierarchy
  • qr experience
  • shelf visibility
  • production system
04go-to-market

building by building.

Rather than mass marketing, the strategy creates dense adoption inside individual residential communities before expanding — messaging, launch sequencing, promo assets and activation plans built to repeat across every future building.

Every touchpoint exists to turn curiosity into habit, because repeat purchase is the true measure of success.

05the result

not a frozen meal. a new retail channel.

BestDish is preparing to launch its first buildings across Toronto — curated restaurant partners, premium packaging, transparent pricing, and a fully developed customer experience built to scale.

One integrated brand experience connecting restaurants, property owners, producers and residents.

06the work

Restaurant partners at launch: Piano Piano, Pukka, Le Gourmand, Death in Venice Gelato Co. — named dishes, named chefs, every pack.
