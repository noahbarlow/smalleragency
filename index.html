01what we solve

brands stall when strategy, package, and demand drift apart.

Most companies don't fail at execution — they fail between functions. The brand deck says one thing, the packaging interprets another, and retail sells whatever's left. We work end to end so the original argument survives the trip from deck to shelf.

No hand-offs between departments, because there are no departments. Just senior people who've made the bet before.

02the framework

brand → package → demand.

The order isn't decorative. Brand sets the bet. Package carries it. Demand pulls it through.

01brand

Positioning sharp enough to be useful inside the building — and durable enough to survive a category shift. Spend elsewhere first and you'll just amplify confusion.

02package

The pack is your most-touched media buy. Hierarchy, semiotics, retail psychology — built to win the three-second test.

03demand

Distribution, GTM sequencing, retail readiness and the content engine that turns trial into preference. In that order.

"packaging is the most under-leveraged growth lever a founder owns. it's media you've already paid for."

— noah barlow, founder, on shelf economics

04the challenger rules

we build challengers.

Ten rules govern how challenger brands win. Here are four we'll defend in any room. Score yourself against all ten with the scorecard below.

rule one

find your enemy

Challengers aren't just different — they fight against something bigger.

rule two

be culturally loud

Don't whisper features. Connect your brand to culture, not just categories.

rule five

be braver than them

Safe is forgettable. Challenger moves should feel risky — and maybe a little scary.

rule ten

stay restless

The second you get comfortable, you become the incumbent.
